Fancy finding yourself in the midst of zombies, the ones who carry chainsaws, because, you know, teeth just aren't enough? Then head to Tokyo. Kowagarasetai, the group that last month brought to fans of the macabre drive-in horror shows, is now putting on 15-minute shows to offer customers a distraction from the pressures of the pandemic. As they lie in a dark room in a coffin with a glass lid listening to tales of terror, customers can forget their future fears as they get prodded by fake zombies and sprayed with water.

"The pandemic is stressful, and we hope people can get a bit of relief by having a good scream," said Kenta Iwana, coordinator of Kowagarasetai, or Scare Squad.

Apparently, it works. Said a satisfied customer: "I feel relaxed now."

It was a stressful four days for Nala and her owner. The cat was on her way from Bengaluru to New Delhi on an Air India flight when the carrier she was traveling in came undone in the aircraft's cargo compartment and the eight-month old slipped out. For four days she managed to elude her rescuers and stayed hidden on the vast premises of Delhi's Indira Gandhi International Airport. Wildlife SOS, a nonprofit called in to assist airport authorities with the search, tried to lure Nala out with food baits, but it was only after four days of tracking that the visibly distressed and hungry cat was spotted in the airport's cargo hold area. It was a few more hours before Nala could be captured and handed over to her relieved owner.
